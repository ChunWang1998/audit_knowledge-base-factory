**Extremely Detailed Accounting Checklist:**
**Rounding / Precision Issues:**
- Arithmetic underflow on negative rebasing tokens (incorrect rounding direction in mulDiv).
- User-favoring vs protocol-favoring rounding in share mint/burn (previewDeposit vs actual deposit).
- Decimal mismatch between different tokens or between vault and strategy contracts.
- Overflow in RebasingLibrary or custom mulDiv without SafeMath.
- Precision loss in fee calculations that compounds over time.
- Rounding direction errors in debt, premium, or risk adjustment calculations (particularly in any update, refresh, or notification paths) that allow repeated small inflations or losses on high-value or low-decimal assets through permissionless or repeated operations. Such errors are especially dangerous when the calculation involves per-operation external calls, when updates are not atomic with share price/NAV/total figures, or when the error can accumulate across many calls on assets with extreme value/decimal characteristics. Pay particular attention to delta or change computations that derive an amount from two sides with inconsistent rounding directions, to explicit small tolerances in requires or comments acknowledging "precision loss", and to repeated permissionless calls that turn small dust into meaningful distortion.
- Precision loss in complex price, boost, valuation, or conversion calculations (especially in strategy, AMO, or multi-step math involving oracles, rates, or external inputs) that compounds or leads to incorrect user-visible amounts, shares, or incentives.

**Share-Price / NAV Calculation Bugs:**
- Stale NAV in maxWithdraw/previewRedeem/previewMint → theft of pending yield.
- totalAssets/totalSupply not updated atomically with rebasing tokens.
- Phantom shares inflation attack (first depositor steals funds).
- Incorrect share price calculation when underlying asset has fee-on-transfer.
- maxWithdraw/tranche logic allows over-withdrawal before NAV update.

**State Desynchronization & Edge Cases:**
- Cross-contract accounting inconsistencies (vault vs strategy balance drift).
- Fee refund precision loss on early withdrawal.
- Tranche maxMint overflow allowing unlimited share minting.
- Rebasing hooks not triggering accounting updates correctly.
- Repeated permissionless operations on state that can accumulate small accounting discrepancies over time.
- Calculations of total debt or supply figures that may omit or mishandle certain components (such as deficits), leading to distorted rates or incentives.
- Aggregates that treat certain amounts (such as deficits or bad debt) as if they were never present, affecting utilization, rates, or user outcomes. In IRM or rate models, deficit ignored in total debt/supply-demand leads to artificially lower rates over time.
- Incorrect proportional fee accrual on deposits/redemptions (late participants receive full or unfair share of accrued fees).
- Redemption/convert amounts that ignore current exchange rate or index.
- Failure to decrement/track withdrawal/redemption/locked pools when assets arrive or are released.
- Specific call sequences (admin/user redemption paths) that leave funds stuck with no recovery.
- Rebasing token support missing or broken, causing permanent accounting mismatch or loss.

**Payout / Reward Distribution Errors (payout-errors/):**
- Hardcoded overpayment values or rounding direction errors enabling grief extraction.
- Reward logic bugs causing incorrect payout amounts (logic-reward/). This includes cases where reward accrual state (such as RewardPerTokenPaid or similar per-user tracking) is re-added or re-initialized, but done incorrectly for some users, leading to those users receiving too many or too few rewards over time.
- Referral reward miscalculations allowing overpayment grief (rewards-referral/).
- Holder yield/payout calculation errors (payouts-holder/).
- Redemption fee disbursement without corresponding collateral backing, leading to depeg or insolvency over time.
- Untracked funds from rejected, cancelled, expired or blacklisted redemptions (or income deposits) that become stuck or are mis-accounted as profit, enabling unbacked issuance.
